One Piece Chapter 1136 Recap

Yamo's Pilgrimage

The chapter kicks off with what I believe to be the cornerstone, and most symbolic entry into Yamato's "Holy Inari Shrine Pilgrimage." At 22 volumes long, it's shaping up to be the longest cover story we've ever had in One Piece, and this particular entry feels more like the pre-cum before climax than any other has thus far.

Who Could It Be?

On my initial read, I quite literally lol'd out loud at the design of the criminal's hideout. Like Geez Louise, Oda!! I wonder just Who's Who the thief could be!? If only there were some sort of ostensible sign outside of the hideout that would give us a clue as to WHO it could be. I'm sure we'll all be very surprised by the reveal of the culprit in the next chapter, because there's just no way anyone could figure it out now!!

All jokes aside, it's obvious that Oda was having a little fun implementing his version of the old cartoon trope; where baddy and superhero hideouts are literally made in their image. It's honestly appalling how long their enemies took to find Xavier's Mansion every time it was attacked... I mean, it was right fucking there... with a golden signpost slapped on the front!! HOW DID ANYONE EVER MISS IT??!! Nonetheless, I digress. As there's also some deeper meaning to the egregiously boisterous hideaway.

On my second read-through of the chapter, I remembered something incredible!! After Who's Who got dishonourably discharged as a CP9 agent, Kaido gave him grace and saved his life. He gave him purpose. A reason to be when he was probably at the lowest point in his life. His name does evoke some sense of an identity crisis, one even removed from the dichotomy of a secret agent with little to no relations outside of work. What this man went through is the fictional equivalent of being a CIA Special Agent tasked with organising the transport of a nuclear warhead, but then some random dude with red hair pulls up and steals it from you. I mean, you'd be one to talk since your hair is hot pink in hue. But you obviously lament the irony in being robbed of a WMD and losing your job to someone with the exact same fashion sense as you... that's gotta be tough to live with.

So considering everything he's been through, it's painfully obvious how much Who's Who really idolised Kaido. And once you see that, you'll realise his hideout isn't just a joke; it was designed to pay homage to the fallen Onigashima. It's Who's Who's little kitty version of what his big dragon daddy had.

But then it goes even further: the way Yamato is pulling up to the spot with her clique is very reminiscent of the way Luffy finally pulled up to Kaido's Roof with his crew.

In my opinion, the biggest point of Yamo's cover story is to have him follow in the exact footsteps of Kouzuki Oden. So while Oden part 2 is on this journey around Wano rounding up various criminals. Just remember that the original Oden's vassals were completely comprised of misfits, the misguided, and people who society considered to be worthless individuals. Pour one out for my guy, Ashura Doji.

Luffy's Dinosaur Meat (not sexual, unless...)

At the start of the chapter proper, Luffy is chowing down on some moose meat like a cow in heat!! And it took every ounce of my willpower not to go searching through 1,000+ chapters of this manga to see when and where the fuck Luffy ate a damn dinosaur. It's such a strange and unexpected declaration that if Sanji was the one to say it, I'd simply assume he had some offscreen fun with Ulti back in Wano Kuni.

Luckily, my soulmate and I had a chat about it, and we concurred that if Luffy was ever depicted eating a dino in One Piece; it could've only happened 1 of 2 times. Either in his backstory growing up in the jungle, where we do know Saber-toothed Tigers lived. Or in Little Garden, where we do know Luffy met a couple of dino bois. So I went looking, and BY GOLLY Luffy really did eat and freaking dinosaur for lunch!! Not only this; the very dino Luffy was eating had previously eaten him. Oda perfectly depicted the Law of Equivalent Exchange and The Cycle of Life in a single manga page and most fans completely forgot about it, shame on us!!

I'm even more ashamed I didn't immediately recall that page in Little Garden, as it was specifically referenced many times during the Egghead Arc. Glaringly, Oda even recreated the scene of Broggy chopping off the dinosaur's head to free Luffy from its tonsils; with the selfsame act being done to the Sand-Worm Gorosei, Saint Shepherd Ju. Peter.

Race Relations at a Party

During the banquet celebrations, a random giant exclaims at Jimbei that his skin "really is blue!!" And I just want to personally thank Oda, for perfectly illustrating how every Black person feels when we have to take a pause and process whether a question is laced with racism before deciding how to answer. This look on Jimbei's face is exactly how every Black man feels whenever some White dude exclaims "wow... you really know how to play ball, don't cha!!"

Just standing there and wondering if this person is genuinely impressed by my mediocre basketball skills, or if I should just tell them to fuck off and get on with my day. Thank you again, Oda, for perfectly illustrating a major part of the Black experience.

Also, Sanji. Please. My Guy. I know they are all giants, but most of them are also all children!! You are in a literal School Zone, Sir!! Please, control your urges!! Trust me when I say your character WILL NOT survive the road you are going down!! We forgave you for Fish-man Island, we forgave you for Dressrosa, we even forgave you putting Robin in mortal danger all because you couldn't stop getting aroused by a lady who was actively trying to kill you. We won't forgive this, Sanji. Not the kids, please... don't do it.

Both Gerd and Rodo are Kinky Giants

Ever since the introduction of the New Giant Warrior Pirates in Egghead. It seemed kinda strange to me how much Gerd kept piling onto Rodo. The very first words we hear out of her mouth as a character are an exclamation of how much she hates Rodo. Practically every single time Rodo's name is even uttered in her earshot after that, she responds unprovoked with some version of "yeah, he should just die already!" If the opportunity ever presents itself to punish Rodo, she is the one leading the charge with her axe. And honestly, I think there's A LOT of self-projection in her actions.

Gerd's sheer disgust towards Rodo comes from a place of guilt she has for possessing what she knows to be the same feelings: a strong desire for some human loving. Who can even blame them as giants? We've really got it going on in the human realm; even the Bible says many angels wanted to suck on some hu... nvm.

The point is, Oda is trying to illustrate the differences in healthy and unhealthy attraction/lust. Rodo goes about expressing his desires in the most carnal and disgusting ways possible. With actual slavery and human trafficking. Gerd, on the other hand, is a little more respectful with her urges, using her spare time to make sexy human-sized clothes JUST IN CASE some humans appear before her in need of clothing. And in her defence, this very unlikely scenario did actually play out; the Straw Hats are very thankful for their Viking drip. But ya know, it's not weird or anything. She just really likes dressing up humans, and I'm guessing many humans also enjoy getting dressed up by her... huh

The Sun God Lore

This is why I love writing! This is why I love reading! This is why I love storytelling! This is why I love manga!! For weeks, fans have been made to scratch their heads as to why this Sun God business was becoming so prevalent all of a sudden. We went from hearing nothing about a Sun God for 800 chapters, then we got a single mention of one in Skypia, and now there's a new character claiming to be the Sun God every single week!! But learning that it's a doctored and misinterpreted legend exclusive to the land of Elbaph makes everything else make sense.

Kuma, Rodo, and Loki all have extremely different interpretations of the Sun God, and it causes each of them to express their faith very differently. Eerily similar to real-life religion, it's worth noting.

Ostensibly, the biggest irony in all of this is the pure beauty of Luffy's idiocy. Of course, every single person on Elbaph right now has their own vision of what the Sun God should be. Everyone, except the one person who IS actually the Sun God's reincarnation. For 800 years, the world has been waiting for the Sun God's next incarnation, but this time it falls to the one person on the planet who couldn't give any less of a fuck... my gosh, I love this man!!

Luffy: *Eats the Sun God fruit* - *Becomes the Sun God incarnate*
Random person with eyes: Hey, are you Nika!? Cause you look a lot like Nika, my guy!!
Luffy: No!! Obviously, the Sun God has nothing to do with me. It's not like I'm his literal third coming or anything.

The Other Sun God

For me, this chapter absolutely confirms that Loki is a good guy at best and an anti-hero archetype at worst. He was either framed by someone else, or even himself, in order to secretly save Elbaf from an unknown fate---I.E. the same thing that happened to Luffy when it was prophesied that he would destroy Fish-man Island. The prophecy wasn't false; its vision was simply misinterpreted.

The giants say that Loki's father, King Herald, prophesied that he would be killed by his own son at least 5 years before Loki was even born. That seems VERY strange to me. That seems like King Herald PLANNED to be killed by his own son. It's also interesting to play your own game of Devil's Advocate with Loki. Reread the chapter and try to come up with your own valid explanations for the named crimes he is accused of committing. I promise, you will surprise yourself by how quickly ALL of them can be explained away with minimal effort. And I believe these crimes were cherry-picked by Oda for this exact reason.

He killed the royal "Lucky Horse" when he was 2? Maybe the damn horse just wasn't that lucky. He's accused of killing the animals in the underworld, but when we visit him there, they all still look very alive, healthy, and he even refers to them as friends. Something definitely is amiss with this Loki narrative; maybe his father was the trickster we're claiming Loki to be. Maybe his father gave him the Alt Sun God Devil Fruit and tasked him with carrying their secret to the grave. All in an effort to save Elbaf from a truth the populace maybe isn't ready for. And what truth could that be...

My Brainrot Sun God Theory

I'll just go right ahead and tell it to you straight. Every single "god" we've met in the One Piece series turned out to be fake. It's almost as if Oda is writing OP with the same narrative the original Scooby-Doo cartoon attached to monsters... underneath the masks and costumes, they're all just humans. From Enel, to Gan Fall, to the gods in Rodo's diorama; every single time we were shown the gods were not real. Why would the Sun God be any different?

What if the reason people believed the Sun God appeared before great worldly change. Is because in the hopelessness of a cataclysmic event, the ancients of the One Piece world looked up to the heavens and saw the clouds to be something they are not. They mistook the sounds of ear-piercing explosions to be uncontrollable godly laughter. It kinda gives me the chills to think about. But for a literal larger-than-the-giants figure, it is a bit peculiar that no one can recall the face, or even facial features on Nika. The only thing people remember about him was that he was always draped in clouds and smiling. Looking to the sky in hopeless situations is a primordial human coping mechanism...

Another interesting thing to support my crack-pot theory. Is the fact that none of the Mythical Zoan Type Devil Fruits have been shown to have counterparts in the real world. Marco is the Mythical Phoenix, but no such bird actually exists in the OP universe. Kaido is the Mythical Azure Dragon, but the Chinese-mythos dragons don't exist in OP either. Matter of fact, the only real dragons we know in One Piece are the artificial ones Vegapunk made using dinosaur DNA, and Luffy's deadbeat father. Even Sengoku has the Devil Fruit of the Mythical Deity, Buddha; but there's just no way Buddha actually exists in One Piece.

So again I ask: why would the Mythical Sun God, Nika, be any different?

Luffy: A Yonko All Grown Up

It came as a pleasant shock to me that Luffy actually confided in the Straw Hats about his secret meeting with Loki. I'm not exactly sure, maybe I'm overthinking it; but Luffy has markedly been acting like a "real captain" more and more, ever so subtly, every single week since the time skip. But in this chapter I was struck with an incredible sense of grown-up responsibility being exuded from shonen's favourite idiot.

Since when did my Rubber Gawd have the wherewithal to inform people more responsible than him about the potentially foolish decisions he makes as a Yonko Captain?? Luffy is now proactively shielding his crew from his natural stupidity. As a Yonko, he has been categorised as an entity that is powerful enough to reshape the world with every decision he makes. Luffy seems to actually understand this, he's no longer acting as if his actions affect he and his crew alone. And this has been the most incredible slab of character development I’ve seen since Sanji started prioritising his friends over tiddies.

The Straw Hat Commanders deciding as a group to free Loki is the best way this could have played out. This is a decision that will have global ramifications, and the crew will be stronger to deal with the consequences of a decision they made it together. Unlike the time Luffy decided to scrap the Going Merry, give all their gold to an underwear-clad dude to build a new ship, and didn’t feel the need to inform anyone on the crew about it 🤦🏾‍♂️

In Summation

As is usually the case, the latest One Piece chapter is undoubtedly the greatest chapter in the series to date. I love this streak that Oda has been on for the past 27 years or so 😌

Elbaph, in my opinion, is going to be one of the longest and most epic arcs in One Piece. It very well should be, the only other island that has been hyped up and teased more throughout the entire series is Laughtale itself. This chapter alone dropped enough lore for Oda to spend the next 100 hashing out; and I absolutely cannot wait to experience all of it ❤️
